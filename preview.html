Avishay Tal

Hi, I'm Avishay, an Associate Professor at the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at UC Berkeley. I am honored to be part of Berkeley's Theory Group.

Main Research Interests

  • Computational Complexity,
  • Analysis of Boolean Functions,
  • Circuit and Formula Lower Bounds,
  • Query Complexity,
  • Pseudorandomness,
  • Computational Learning Theory,
  • Quantum Computing,
  • Combinatorics, and
  • Connections between Algorithms and Lower Bounds.

Motivating questions that guide my research:

  • What is the power and limitations of parallel computation?
  • What is the role of randomness in computation?
  • What is the role of memory in learning?
  • For which tasks do quantum algorithms outperform classical algorithms?

Short Bio

Short Bio: I received my Ph.D. in 2015 from the Weizmann Institute of Science, under the guidance of Ran Raz. Prior to that, I was an M.Sc. student at the Technion under the guidance of Amir Shpilka until 2012. I held postdoctoral appointments at the Institute for Advanced Study (hosted by Avi Wigderson) from 2015 to 2017, Simons Institute (as part of the Lower Bounds in Computational Complexity program), and Stanford University (hosted by Omer Reingold) from 2017 to 2019. I feel very fortunate to have had Amir, Ran, Avi, and Omer as my mentors. I joined the EECS department at UC Berkeley in 2019.
